## Brand Identity

**Brand Name:** JDM Connect
**Brand Line:** をつなぐ / CONNECT (Connecting JDM)
**Full Japanese Brand Line:** ジェー・ディー・エムをつなぐ

**Brand Essence Statement:**
JDM Connect exists to connect authentic Japanese Domestic Market vehicles and culture with discerning global owners through trust, expertise, and intentional curation. The brand is defined not by volume or transaction, but by the quality of connection it creates.

**Brand Purpose:**
To bridge the gap between the depth, heritage, and integrity of Japanese automotive culture and the expectations of an international audience seeking confidence, clarity, and authenticity. Its purpose is to enable access responsibly, translating complexity into confidence while preserving provenance.

**Brand Role:**
JDM Connect operates as a Connector — not a marketplace or volume-driven importer. Value lies in providing access, curation, and trust. By focusing on relationships rather than transactions, JDM Connect positions itself as a trusted bridge between Japanese automotive culture and discerning global owners.

## Brand Personality — We Are / We Are Not

| We Are | We Are Not |
|--------|-----------|
| **Confident, not loud** — Language is measured, precise, and intentional | Aggressive or over-promotional |
| **Curated, not commercial** — Options presented selectively, valuing quality and provenance over abundance or urgency | Loud or volume-driven |

## Brand Tone

JDM Connect's tone reflects quiet confidence, cultural fluency, and deliberate restraint. Communication is clear, considered, and authoritative — never loud, rushed, or sales-driven. The tone should feel like **guidance from an expert, not persuasion from a seller.**

### Tone Principles

- **Measured and confident** — Language is calm and assured. Statements are made with clarity, without exaggeration or hype.
- **Clear, not complicated** — Complex processes are explained simply and precisely. Jargon is avoided unless it adds genuine value.
- **Respectful and culturally aware** — Japanese terms are used thoughtfully and sparingly, reinforcing authenticity rather than novelty.
- **Transparent and reassuring** — Information is delivered openly, building trust through honesty rather than urgency.
- **Minimal, not sparse** — Words are chosen carefully. Every sentence serves a purpose.

### Tone in Practice ✅
- Use direct, well-structured sentences
- Prefer clarity over cleverness
- Explain when necessary, then step back
- Allow space — visually and verbally
- Let expertise lead the conversation

### What the Tone Is Not ❌
- Loud or aggressive
- Casual, slang-heavy, or jokey
- Sales-driven or urgency-based
- Overly technical or condescending

## Language & Terminology

- Use "JDM Connect" (not "JDM" alone when referring to the brand)
- The brand line ジェー・ディー・エムをつなぐ / をつなぐ must never be rewritten, rearranged, or substituted
- Japanese terms used thoughtfully and sparingly as signals of authenticity
- Avoid slang, filler phrases, urgency language ("limited time", "act now", etc.)
- Avoid overly promotional superlatives

## Typography

- **Logo wordmark:** Custom JDM letterforms — never recreate, substitute, or approximate
- **"CONNECT" in logo:** Nulshock — used only in supplied logo artwork, not for body or headings
- **Primary English typeface (body/headings):** Inter (Option 1), Helvetica (Option 2), or Manrope (Option 3)
- **Japanese brand line:** Microsoft YaHei UI Bold
- **Hierarchy:** Headings Medium–SemiBold / Subheadings Regular–Medium / Body Regular / Captions Light–Regular
- **Capitalisation:** Title or sentence case for headings; sentence case for body copy; avoid full uppercase paragraphs
- Generous whitespace; avoid crowding text blocks

## Visual Identity Principles

- Precision over excess — elements feel intentional and refined
- Strong contrast and clarity — especially in motion or real-world environments
- Minimalism with purpose — avoid visual noise or unnecessary decoration
- Consistency across touchpoints — every interaction feels unmistakably JDM Connect
- Dark backgrounds enhance contrast, depth, and premium automotive aesthetic
- Gold tones used with intention — reserved for emphasis, brand marks, and key visual moments

---

## Content Do's and Don'ts Summary

**Do:**
- Write with authority and calm confidence
- Lead with expertise, follow with context
- Keep copy concise and purposeful
- Reference Japanese culture meaningfully when relevant
- Emphasise quality, curation, provenance, and relationship

**Don't:**
- Use urgency-based language or promotional pressure
- Be casual, jokey, or use heavy slang
- Over-explain or condescend
- Use the brand culturally as decoration or trend
- Expand beyond approved brand expressions
